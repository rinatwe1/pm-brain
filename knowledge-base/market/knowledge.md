# Market Knowledge

## Competitive Analysis Framework
**What it is:** Structured comparison of competitors across positioning, pricing, features, and target segments.
**Core insight:** Competitors reveal what the market has validated. Gaps reveal where value is unclaimed.
**PM use case:** Before any major feature or pivot, map where you sit relative to alternatives. Update quarterly or when a competitor ships something significant.

## Crossing the Chasm (Moore)
**What it is:** The gap between early adopters and mainstream buyers — the most dangerous transition in a product's lifecycle.
**Core insight:** Early adopters buy vision. Mainstream buyers buy proof. You cannot cross the chasm with the same message or channel.
**PM use case:** When growth stalls after initial traction, diagnose whether you're stuck in the chasm. The fix is a narrow beachhead — one segment, fully served.

## Category Creation vs Category Entry
**What it is:** You either enter an existing market, reframe an existing market, or create a new category.
**Core insight:** Category creators set the terms of comparison. Category entrants compete on those terms. Most PMs default to entry when creation was available.
**PM use case:** Define your category explicitly. Who sets the frame? If you don't, competitors and customers will.

## Jobs Map (Ulwick)
**What it is:** A structured inventory of the functional, emotional, and social jobs customers hire a product to do.
**Core insight:** Customers don't buy products — they hire them to make progress. Unserved jobs are market opportunities.
**PM use case:** Map all jobs in your domain, score satisfaction and importance, find the quadrant of important + underserved. That's your opportunity space.

## Network Effects and Defensibility
**What it is:** Products where value increases as more users join — platform, marketplace, and social dynamics.
**Core insight:** Network effects are the strongest moat, but they only activate at scale. The hardest problem is getting to that threshold.
**PM use case:** Identify whether your product has network effects. If yes, optimize for supply-demand balance and cold-start mechanics. If no, identify your actual defensibility.
