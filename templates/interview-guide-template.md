## Interview Structure

### Introduction (5 min)
- Thank them for their time
- Explain purpose: "We're exploring [problem area] and want to understand your experience"
- Consent: "Is it okay if I record/take notes?"
- Set expectations: "No right/wrong answers, just want to hear your honest experience"

---

## Section 1: Background (5-10 min)

**Goal:** Understand who they are and their context

1. **Role & Context**
   - Can you tell me about your role/what you do?
   - How long have you been [doing X]?

2. **Current Workflow**
   - Walk me through a typical [day/week/process]
   - What does your workflow look like?

---

## Section 2: Pain Points (10-15 min)

**Goal:** Identify problems and frustrations

3. **Current Challenges**
   - What's the hardest part about [activity]?
   - What frustrates you most about [current solution]?
   - Tell me about a time when [problem] caused issues for you

4. **Workarounds**
   - How do you currently deal with [problem]?
   - What tools/methods do you use?
   - What's missing from existing solutions?

5. **Impact**
   - How much time does [problem] cost you?
   - What happens if you can't solve [problem]?
   - How does this affect [business/life/work]?

---

## Section 3: Needs & Desires (10-15 min)

**Goal:** Understand what they actually want

6. **Ideal Solution**
   - If you could wave a magic wand, what would the perfect solution look like?
   - What would make [activity] 10x easier?

7. **Must-Haves vs Nice-to-Haves**
   - What features would you absolutely need?
   - What would be nice but not essential?

8. **Decision Criteria**
   - What would make you switch to a new solution?
   - What concerns would you have about trying something new?
   - Who else is involved in this decision?

---

## Section 4: Context & Discovery (5-10 min)

**Goal:** Understand their environment and discovery process

9. **Current Tools**
   - What tools do you use now for [activity]?
   - What do you like/dislike about them?

10. **How They Find Tools**
    - How do you usually discover new tools/solutions?
    - Who do you trust for recommendations?

11. **Willingness to Pay**
    - Is this something you/your company would pay for?
    - What's a reasonable price range?

---

## Section 5: Specific Product Concept (5 min)

**Optional - only if you have a specific concept to validate**

12. **Concept Validation**
    - [Describe the product concept briefly]
    - What's your initial reaction?
    - What would you use it for?
    - What concerns do you have?

---

## Closing (2-5 min)

13. **Wrap-Up**
    - Is there anything I didn't ask that you think is important?
    - Do you know others who might have insights on this?
    - Can I follow up if I have more questions?

14. **Thank You**
    - Thank them for their time
    - Mention next steps (if any)

## Tips for Good Interviews

### Do:
- ✅ Ask "why" and "tell me more"
- ✅ Listen 80%, talk 20%
- ✅ Ask about specific past experiences ("Tell me about the last time...")
- ✅ Stay curious and neutral
- ✅ Take detailed notes or record (with permission)

### Don't:
- ❌ Lead the witness ("Wouldn't you love a feature that...")
- ❌ Pitch your solution
- ❌ Ask hypothetical questions ("Would you use...")
- ❌ Defend or explain
- ❌ Ask yes/no questions
