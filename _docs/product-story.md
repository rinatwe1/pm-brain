# PM Brain — Product Story

## The Problem

Claude forgets everything. Every session starts from scratch.

A PM working with Claude today faces a daily context tax: re-explaining why a feature was killed last sprint, re-stating tech constraints, re-briefing on competitor positioning. After 90 days, that PM has told Claude the same things hundreds of times — and Claude still doesn't know.

The deeper problem: a PM's institutional knowledge — why decisions were made, what was invalidated, what patterns emerged — lives in their head. Not in the tool. Not in the team.

## The Vision

When PM Brain is successful, Claude acts like a senior team member who was in every meeting.

A PM opens Claude Code in their product directory. Without being asked, Claude knows: "We decided not to build X because of Y — should this initiative reconsider that?" It surfaces an expired hypothesis before a sprint starts. It applies discovery rules that were validated six months ago.

After 90 days of use, the PM stops re-explaining context and starts having a different quality of conversation — one where Claude challenges assumptions rather than just accepting them.

## The Narrative

PM Brain is invisible infrastructure. Like git.

You install it once, from GitHub, with three commands. Then you forget it exists. You open your product directory in Claude Code, type `/brain-init`, answer a few questions — and Claude starts accumulating memory.

Every decision you make gets logged. Every hypothesis you test gets tracked. Every session ends with Claude updating a SNAPSHOT of where the product stands. By month three, you have a Claude that knows your product better than any new team member ever could.

The memory lives in `.pm-brain/` — a hidden folder inside your product directory. It travels with the codebase. It's committed to git. It's yours.

## The Differentiator

Every competitor either:
- Gives Claude skills but no memory (AI-SHIPR — 20+ skills, stateless)
- Adds memory for personal preferences, not product knowledge (ChatGPT Memory)
- Lives outside the PM's workflow (Squad AI, Notion AI)

PM Brain is the only tool that gives Claude **longitudinal, product-specific memory** that accumulates over time and lives in the PM's own IDE.

Backed by Pawel Huryn's validated architecture: Knowledge Architecture + Decision Journal + Quality Gate — three blocks that make Claude measurably smarter every session.

## The Positioning

**What it is:** Git for product knowledge.
**Who it's for:** Technical PMs who use Claude Code daily and are tired of re-explaining context.
**What it's not:** A PM tool. A web app. A replacement for AI-SHIPR. A course.
